## 3. Empirical Research: The Vanishing Gradient Problem
My most significant technical contribution was the numerical proof of the Vanishing Gradient problem across different activation regimes:

Experiment: I captured the gradient norms of the first linear layer (closest to the input) during the initial backward pass to observe signal attenuation.

Comparative Findings:

* Sigmoid: Showed the most extreme signal loss (lowest norm), proving the "saturation" problem.
* Tanh: Performed better than Sigmoid due to its zero-centered nature, providing a more balanced gradient flow, yet still suffered from saturation in deep layers.
* ReLU: Maintained the strongest signal, as it does not saturate in the positive domain.

Conclusion: I mathematically proved that ReLU provides the most stable learning signal, which is fundamental for optimizing deep architectures.
